This interaction is built to follow the prompt: 'Translucent' and 'Agency'. During the design research phase, this work was greatly influenced by the 'glass morphism' After Effect animation trend. While creating the idea and studying on the site YouTube, I came across instructions for the glass morph effect. And, while the tutorials I discovered were mostly about After Effects, they encouraged me to design the first concept of what the interaction would be.

The central interaction is designed to allow others to interact with the clip element ‘button’--a cursor-controlled movable object– that act as a peeking or looking glass of a layer that otherwise is covered by an overlay semi-transparent layer.

The first thing I did to start the interaction was to create a background layer and a mask element as the object. To create the mask, I explored W3Schools and StackOverflow since I am not that familiar with either HTML or CSS, moreover, javascript. I found two ways to implement the mask which were to create a mask image or use a clip path.

A mask image allowed me to make a radial gradient mask that made a transparent circle whilst covering the rest of the page with a semi-transparent gradient. Whilst exploring the mask image action, I also found the mix blend mode command to blend the mask and control the transparency options. Using a mask image was closer aesthetically to the vision I had in my head. However, even after weeks of trials, I did not find any way to connect the mask to a moveable object, especially since the mask itself covered the entire page, and moving it around will result in moving the entire background (clipped image) with it.

The second option, the clip path, allowed me to make a clipping circle that I can move around and connect to javascript. However, since it is a clip and not a mask, I could not make the rest of the background layer semi-transparent.

I chose to combine the clip path and the mix blend mode actions to tackle both of these issues. I kept the clip path I made as the main piece that users may move around and added an overlay layer to represent a semi-transparent backdrop picture underneath it. I also opted to place this text within the clip path so that users have a clear motive to move the element around in order to display the text beneath.

Upon landing on the page, Users can see a peek of the heading title as a hint of layer and content underneath. As a respond to the prompt agency, a clear procedure is implemented in the whole interaction. When users hover over the element, the cursor changed to grab as a hint to click the element. While clicking the element, the element becomes a larger clip and the cursor changed again into a move cursor. The move cursor hint for the next part of the procedure: dragging the element around to reveal a clear image of the background and content.
